User Guide

Application: IconAlbums
Effective Date: 2025-12-08
Last Updated: 2025-12-08

1. What IconAlbums Does

IconAlbums lets you curate small, icon-sized albums and pin them to your launcher for quick access. Each album stores the photos you choose, a cropped icon cover, and a slideshow-ready gallery.

2. Album List Screen

  • The main screen displays your albums in a two-column grid.
  • Tap an album tile to open it. An empty state appears when you have no albums yet.
  • Use the ＋ floating button to create a new album. Names are generated automatically and can be renamed later from the detail screen’s toolbar menu.

3. Creating Albums & Free Tier Limits

Free users can keep up to 3 albums, each containing 20 photos. Purchasing IconAlbums Premium removes both limits. If you reach a limit, the app prompts you to upgrade.

4. Adding Photos

  1. Open an album and tap Add photos.
  2. On Android 13 (API 33) and above, the system Photo Picker appears. On Android 12 and below, the Storage Access Framework picker opens. Select one photo at a time.
  3. Chosen photos appear in the grid instantly. To remove one, tap the trash icon on its card.

Photo metadata is stored locally along with the URI returned by the picker. The originals remain wherever they are stored.

5. Setting the Album Icon

  • Tap any photo in the grid to launch the square crop tool (powered by uCrop).
  • Adjust the crop and confirm. The resulting image becomes the album’s launcher icon and cover.
  • The cropped icon is saved to the app’s private storage. Deleting the app removes these generated files.

6. Viewing Photos

Two display modes are available inside an album:

ModeHow to use it
GridDefault layout for managing photos. Use the remove button or tap a tile to crop.
PagerTap Show pager to switch to a full-width carousel. Swipe left/right to browse.

When the pager is visible, tap Start slideshow to pick a 3, 10, or 20 second interval. The slideshow loops until you tap Stop slideshow or switch back to the grid.

7. Pinning Shortcuts

  • Tap Pin shortcut in the album header to request a launcher shortcut.
  • Approve the launcher prompt. The shortcut uses your cropped icon and opens the album directly.
  • Once pinned, the button changes to Pinned. You can remove the shortcut later from your launcher.

8. Settings & Premium

Open Settings from the toolbar on the album list.

  • Purchase Premium starts the Google Play purchase flow.
  • Restore purchases re-queries Google Play to unlock Premium on a new device.
  • Mock billing is a developer toggle for testing and has no effect in production builds.
  • Privacy policy, terms of service, licenses, and this guide open in the app’s built-in HTML viewer for offline access.

9. Tips & Troubleshooting

  • If a photo cannot be loaded later, re-open the picker and add it again. The app needs continued access to the source URI.
  • For Android 12 or lower, ensure storage permissions are granted when requested. Without permission the picker cannot provide files.
  • To reset the app, clear its data or uninstall it. This removes albums, photo references, and cached icons.
  • If the launcher declines to pin a shortcut, long-press the home screen and look for the Widgets section to add it manually.
